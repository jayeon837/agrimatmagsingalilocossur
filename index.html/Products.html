Magsingal Tinubong

The tinubong is the 'One Town, One Product' of Magsingal. Tinubong means cooking a mixture of young coconut meat, sugar, margarine, cheese, and ground glutinous rice inside a young bamboo or tubong. This native rice delicacy is a favorite among the Ilocanos, which they usually serve during special occasions such as birthdays, Christmas, and New Year.

Magsingal Empanada

Magsingal empanada is also one of the best products of Magsingal. One of the best empanadas is from Lucky and Neo's empanada, located near the Magsingal Belfry and Museum because they have a unique sauce called the 'dinuguan sauce' or, in Ilocano, its 'dara'.

Rice Production

Agriculture dominates the municipality, occupying 45.56 percent of the total land area. This includes rice, corn, vegetables, tobacco, root crops, and fruit trees. Hilly areas have been converted to crop production, while agro-forest land covers 40.59 percent, used for bamboo, fruit, and non-fruit tree production in various parts.

Clay Pots or Lusob

Although they originated in Magsingal, clay pots and other goods really took off in Vigan. Ilocano's clay pots are lusob. Actually, even before, they get supplies from Vigan. Sadly, the location where the Magsingal people obtain their clay is too deep, therefore the items there did not flourish when they attempted to make clay products at that time.
